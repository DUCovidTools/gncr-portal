Report 8 (Week 25/05/2020)

HIGHLIGHTS

  • The main theme this week is that, despite two weeks of a slow departure from lockdown almost all of the authority districts in the North East regions have flattened their curves.
    • This is true for all trends, from the slowest to the worst!
    • New cases have also slowed down (see Figure 2 below).
  • Leeds, County Durham and Sheffield – the three starkest trends – continue to flatten This is particularly true for Sheffield.
  • Overall, the majority of authority districts (n=16) in the North East regions remain part of the slowest trends in England and Italy.

MORE SPECIFIC TRENDS

  1. Over the past week, the total number of authority districts in the North East regions in the slowest curves in England and Italy stayed roughly the same at n=16 (roughly 59%). Following last week, most of the trends continue to remain relatively flat; however, a few, such as Kingston upon Hull, have had a slight uptick.
  2. The daily number of new cases (over the last two weeks) across all n=27 authority districts support this general insight, with many slowing down and levelling. As further demonstration, a trend line was plotted for Gateshead and County Durham, which show the number of new cases does seem to be slowing down and have passed the peak.
  3. Bradford continue to also be part of the slow to moderate growing trends. But, while higher than the slowest trends, Bradford was going up as of last week. This week it appears to be levelling off slightly.
  4. Moderate trends for Sunderland, Newcastle, North Yorkshire, Rotherham and Northumberland, North Yorkshire, Gateshead and Barnsley all appear to have flattened.
  5. Leeds and County Durham continue to level off. While two of the curves with the highest numbers in the North East, North Yorkshire and Humber regions, relative to the entire England and Italy dataset they are not part of the worst curves.
  6. Even Sheffield has levelled off and continues to be relatively flat, despite being the most severe trend in the North East and North Yorkshire.
Report 7 (Week 18/05/2020)

HIGHLIGHTS

  • The main theme this week is that the majority of authority districts in the North East, North Yorkshire and Humber regions continue to be levelling off.
    • This is true for all trends, from the slowest to the worst!
    • New cases have also slowed down (see Figure 2 below).
  • Leeds, County Durham and Sheffield – the three worst trends – are finally levelling off.
  • Overall, the majority of authority districts (n=17) in the North East and North Yorkshire and Humber regions remain part of the slowest trends in England and Italy.

MORE SPECIFIC TRENDS

  1. Over the past week, the total number of authority districts in the North East regions in the slowest curves in England and Italy stayed roughly the same at n=16 (roughly 59%). Following last week, most of the trends continue to remain relatively flat; however, a few, such as Kingston upon Hull, have had a slight uptick.
  2. The daily number of new cases (over the last two weeks) across all n=27 authority districts support this general insight, with many slowing down. As further demonstration, a trend line was plotted for County Durham, which shows the number of new cases does seem to be slowing down.
  3. Bradford continue to also be part of the slow to moderate growing trends. But, while higher than the slowest trends, Bradford continues to increase in cases.
  4. Sunderland, Newcastle, North Yorkshire, Rotherham and Northumberland, North Yorkshire, Gateshead and Barnsley are all part of the moderate trends, but also all appear to be slowing down as well and levelling off.
  5. Leeds and County Durham seem to finally be levelling off. Nonetheless, they remain two of the curves with the highest numbers in the North East and North Yorkshire regions. But relative to the entire England and Italy dataset they are not part of the worst curves.
  6. Even Sheffield has levelled off, despite being the most severe trend in the North East and North Yorkshire.

About the Durham COVID-19 Modelling team

The COVID-19 Community Health and Social Care Modelling Team is under the guidance of our respective Executive Deans, Jacqui Ramagge (Science) and Charlotte Clarke (Social Sciences and Health) as part of the contribution of the Wolfson Research Institute for Health and Wellbeing and the Institute of Data Sciences to the University’s Health@Durham strategy, as well as supported by the Durham Advanced Research Computing team, Research and Innovation Services, Marketing and Communications and CECAN (Centre for the study of Complexity Across the Nexus).

The team is led by Dr Camila Caiado and Professor Brian Castellani, with the purpose of creating a series of tools and dashboards that Trusts and Councils can use to help support decision and planning accordingly. Some of these tools are hosted by Durham University through IBM’s cloud, which they have provided presently for free as part of our Academic Partnership. We would also like to acknowledge the outstanding contribution of Dr Jennifer Badham, Dr Peter Barbrook-Johnson, Professor Amanda Ellison, Dr Andrew Iskauskas, Dr Bernard Piette, Dr Rachel Oughton, and Dr Corey Schimpf.

Please contact Camila Caiado with any queries.
